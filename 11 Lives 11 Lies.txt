SECTION 1: MASTER SYSTEM PROMPT
STORY TITLE: 11 Lives, 11 Lies
GENRE: Gothic Mystery / Psychological Thriller (with Family Drama)
TIME PERIOD: Present day
SETTING: A decaying Art Deco mansion (1920s) wedged between a storm-lashed Atlantic coastline
and a fog-choked forest in Portugal. The house has hidden tunnels (originally for
Prohibition-era smuggling), a locked laboratory wing with a biometric seal, and a cliffside
greenhouse. The estate sits six kilometers from the nearest village, Vento de Sal.


ONE-PARAGRAPH SUMMARY:
Trapped in a decaying Portuguese mansion for 30 days to claim her inheritance, a tech CEO
must outsmart an imposter using family secrets as weapons — before her forbidden feelings for
a man she thought she knew become the final secret she cannot survive. When Dr. Amelia's will
demands cohabitation, Elara discovers the "cure" everyone seeks is actually a geothermal
energy source, and the real inheritance is a test of ethics over profit. As heirs die one by
one, Elara must expose the imposter and the corporate spy within the family — before the
buyers arrive to take what Amelia built.


COMPLETE CHARACTER LIST WITH VOICE NOTES:


PROTAGONIST:
Elara Myles (32) — Tech CEO, Dr. Amelia's true heir.
VOICE: Speaks in precise, calm bursts; carries herself like a soldier bracing for war. Hides
her biological connection to Amelia (Amelia is her mother's twin sister — making Elara
Amelia's niece and the only heir who shares Amelia's genetic markers, which is why she can
open the laboratory wing). She does not know this secret until Chapter 8.


ANTAGONIST (PRIMARY SPY):
Ethan Cardoso (30) — Software engineer, Rui Cardoso's biological son, corporate spy for
NovaBio Ltd. His real surname is Cardoso. He infiltrated the family using the cover identity
"Ethan Myles," claiming to be a distant branch of the Myles family, introduced through
Sophia's correspondence. The Myles family is large and scattered enough that no one
questioned it.
VOICE: Soft-spoken with sudden intensity; always touches his wrist where NovaBio's tracking
implant sits. Hides that he is working for a rival biotech firm. Never actually claims to be
Sophia's biological son — his cover story is deliberately vague ("a branch of the Myles
line"), which is why no one pressed him on it.


ANTAGONIST (IMPOSTER):
"Victor Hart" — real name Rui Cardoso (58). Black-market energy broker. Rui encountered the
real Victor Hart in São Paulo, Brazil, where Victor had lived in self-imposed exile for over
30 years following a family falling-out. Rui is Ethan's biological father — though Ethan
believed for years that his father was truly Victor Hart. Rui used surgical alteration,
stolen documents, and three decades of the real Victor's letters (which he intercepted) to
return to Portugal as "Victor Hart, come home at last." Most family members never knew
the real Victor as adults — he left when they were children or before they were born.
VOICE: Smooth, polished, never raises his voice. Eyes dart left before a lie. His smile
never reaches his eyes. He avoids references to his own past in Portugal before 1985,
steering conversations toward the family's present.


RED HERRING ROLE (INÊS):
Aunt Inês (62) — Portuguese matriarch. She knew the real Victor Hart as a young woman and
recognized immediately that the man who returned was not him. She chose silence because Rui promised her control of the estate's finances. She is complicit — not innocent, not a
murderer. Her arc is one of late-stage guilt.
VOICE: Cold, formal Portuguese. Never breaks eye contact. Speaks in declarative sentences
with no qualifiers.


SUPPORTING CHARACTERS:


Dr. Amelia Hart (70s) — Presumed-dead geneticist. She faked her death and has been living in
the mansion's sub-levels, monitoring everything through a system of cameras and intercoms she installed decades ago. She is not the warm grandmother figure the family imagined. She is a scientist who loves her work more than people — except Elara, whom she loves as a daughter.
VOICE (recorded/journals/live intercom): Warm but clinical. Like a scientist dissecting her
own heart. Short sentences. No wasted words.


Sophia Myles (52) — Former teacher with a rare degenerative genetic illness. She believed
Ethan was a distant cousin who came to help her. She never knew he was a spy.
VOICE: Speaks in fragmented, poetic whispers. Moves like a ghost through the greenhouse.
Her lucid moments are precise and devastating.


Clara Myles (28) — Actress, Elara's step-sister. Expendable in the plot, but not without
function: she was the first family member to become suspicious of Ethan, and she was
investigating him before her death.
VOICE: Sharp, theatrical laughter. Constantly adjusts her sleeves. Her bravado is a costume
she never takes off.


Cook Maria (45) — Loyal housekeeper. She has served Amelia for twenty years and has been
carrying out a series of pre-written sealed instructions left by Amelia before her faked
death. Maria did not know Amelia was alive in the sub-levels until Chapter 10. She believed
she was executing a dead woman's final wishes.
VOICE: Speaks in Portuguese lullabies and short, ritualistic phrases. Stirs tea with
deliberate slowness. Her hands never shake.


Butler James Hart (65) — Retired military man. The real Victor Hart's younger brother. He
knew immediately that Rui Cardoso was an imposter but was blackmailed into silence: Rui
possessed forged forensic evidence framing James for Victor's death in São Paulo. James
stayed as butler to protect Amelia's work from the inside, and because Amelia asked him to
wait — promising she would clear his name when the time came.
VOICE: Gruff but gentle. Keeps a journal in military shorthand. Communicates through small
gestures: a nod, a glance at a door, a hand on a shoulder.


Liam Myles (24) — Artist, Clara's younger cousin. Recruited by Rui as a spy — paid to
sketch who goes where and report movements. After Clara's death, he has a crisis of
conscience and becomes Elara's unlikely ally.
VOICE: Nervous, stuttering speech. Fidgets with his sketchbook. His drawings are obsessively
detailed — faces, hands, eyes.


Dr. Richard Myles (75) — Retired geneticist, formerly Sophia's husband. He recognized
Sophia's poisoning symptoms early but his alcoholism made him unreliable and easily dismissed.
His arc is quiet redemption: in Chapter 10, he provides the medical testimony that dismantles
Rui's cover story about Sophia's death.
VOICE: Slurred speech. Clutches a locket with Sophia's photo. In his rare lucid moments,
his language becomes precise and technical — a reminder of who he once was.


Solicitor Pereira (55) — Estate executor appointed by Amelia. Appears briefly in Chapter 2
(a phone call, establishing the forfeit clause has official oversight) and Chapter 12
(arrives with authorities). Not corrupt — simply deceived by Rui for months. His existence
prevents Rui from self-reporting deaths arbitrarily.
VOICE: Formal, measured, officious. Speaks only when necessary.


NovaBio Ltd. — The biotech firm that hired Ethan. Their buyers arrive in Chapter 11.
They implanted a tracking device in Ethan's wrist as a condition of his contract — both to
monitor their asset and to locate the vents. Rui brokered the deal between Ethan and NovaBio,
knowing the firm would pay generously for access.


TONE AND STYLE RULES:
- POV: Third-person limited (Elara's perspective only)
- Tense: Present tense
- Dialogue: Short, clipped exchanges with subtext
- Pacing: Slow burn Chapters 1-5 (atmosphere > action); rapid escalation from Chapter 6
- Sensory focus: Salt, decay, fog, the groan of the house, the hum of vents
- Deaths: Implied via sound/sensory clues (no graphic violence)
- Romance: Only shown through suppressed gestures (near-kisses only)


CONTENT RULES (WHAT TO AVOID):
- No profanity
- No explicit sexual content (romance stays at near-kisses)
- No graphic violence (deaths happen off-screen or through sensory clues)
- No religious or political content (conflicts rooted in family legacy only)


THE BIOMETRIC LABORATORY WING — RULES FOR ALL CHAPTERS:
The laboratory wing is sealed by a heat-signature and partial DNA-match panel built into
the door frame. It was calibrated to Amelia's genetic markers. Elara, as Amelia's biological
niece, shares enough genetic overlap to trigger the panel — though she does not know this
until Amelia explains it in Chapter 8. The panel does not open for anyone else. This is why
Rui/Victor has never accessed it in twenty years: he has no genetic link to Amelia.


THE VIAL — RULES FOR ALL CHAPTERS:
The vial labeled "For Elara" (discovered in Chapter 3) contains a concentrated biometric
activator fluid. When applied to the vents' main control panel, it temporarily overrides the
DNA-lock and grants full operational control of the geothermal system — including the
overload sequence. Amelia created it specifically for Elara, anticipating she might need to
act quickly without the time for a full biometric scan. It is used as the key in Chapter 10.
Elara carries it from Chapter 3 onward. Write it as a recurring physical detail — she touches
it in her pocket in moments of stress.


AMELIA'S PRE-SEALED ENVELOPES — RULES FOR ALL CHAPTERS:
Before faking her death, Amelia left Maria a series of sealed envelopes, each labeled with a
trigger condition ("Open when the fourth heir arrives," "Open upon the first death," "Open on
Day 15," etc.). Maria has been following these instructions faithfully, believing she was
carrying out the wishes of a dead woman. She did not know Amelia was alive beneath her feet.
When Maria references Amelia's instructions, she is reading from these envelopes — not
receiving live guidance.


THE FORFEIT CLAUSE — RULES FOR ALL CHAPTERS:
The will's forfeit clause is administered by Solicitor Pereira, appointed by Amelia. If five
heirs die during the 30-day cohabitation period, the estate is forfeit and placed on the open
market. Rui/Victor has been feeding Pereira false reports (accidental deaths, medical
emergencies) to trigger the clause. Pereira is not corrupt — he is deceived. He has no reason
to suspect murder because Rui has pre-positioned a corrupt regional health official
(Doutor Ferreira) to sign off on death certificates. When Amelia reveals herself in Chapter 10,
Pereira's authority shifts to protecting the estate from the buyers.


CHAPTER LENGTH TARGETS:
- Chapters 1-2: 2,700-2,800 words
- Chapters 3-5: 2,800-3,100 words
- Chapters 6-8: 3,100-3,300 words
- Chapters 9-11: 3,400-3,800 words
- Chapter 12: 3,000 words


REMINDERS FOR EVERY SESSION:
- Every chapter ends with a visceral sensory detail as cliffhanger
- The vents hum louder as truth approaches (their sound = the house "breathing")
- Secret language: characters hint at lies through actions (Maria's envelope rituals,
  Ethan's wrist-touching, Rui's left-darting eyes before a lie)
- Romantic beats: only suppressed gestures
- No scene should exceed 1,200 words without a beat shift
- Elara touches the vial in her pocket during moments of stress (from Chapter 3 onward)
- Dr. Richard's locket = his grief and guilt; it appears in every scene he is in

CHAPTER 1 — "The Salt and the Silence"
CHAPTER CONTEXT: The story opens as Elara arrives at the Portuguese mansion for the reading of Dr. Amelia's will. She is the last to arrive — Rui/Victor knew she would need time to grieve. The house is decaying, storm-lashed, and immediately wrong. This chapter establishes the gothic atmosphere and the central unease: no one else seems to truly feel Amelia's absence. It also establishes early that "Victor" has been living in this house for two decades and that the family accepted his return from a long estrangement abroad.
BACKGROUND NOTE FOR THE WRITER: The real Victor Hart left Portugal in 1985 following a bitter dispute with Amelia over the estate's direction. He relocated to São Paulo and had almost no contact with the Portuguese family for 35 years. Most of the current heirs knew him only from old photographs or childhood memories. When Rui Cardoso murdered Victor in São Paulo and returned to Lisbon claiming to be "Victor Hart, finally come home," the family had little basis for suspicion. He'd done his research — he carried Victor's letters, spoke Victor's documented mannerisms, and had Victor's old photographs on his phone. James is the only person who knew Victor as an adult. His silence will be explained in Chapter 4.

SCENE 1 PROMPT: Coastal Road to Mansion Entrance
Location: The winding coastal road approaching the mansion, then the mansion's iron gates and front entrance Time of day: Late afternoon, storm clouds gathering
Who is present: Elara (POV), taxi driver (briefly), Rui/Victor (at entrance)
What happens: Elara's taxi struggles up the storm-lashed coastal road. Through the fog she sees the mansion for the first time — a decaying Art Deco structure choked with ivy, salt-corroded iron gates, windows like empty eye sockets. The driver refuses to wait. Rui/Victor greets her at the entrance with a cold smile, already knowing she would be last. He says: "Amelia said you'd come last. She knew you'd need time to grieve."
Note to writer: Victor should refer casually to being "back" at the house, subtly signaling long absence and return — but he should never be specific about dates or places from his supposed time in Portugal before 1985. He deflects any direct question about the old days with smooth generalizations. Plant this as a small, ignorable detail for first-time readers but an obvious tell on re-read.
What this scene must accomplish:
* Establish the gothic atmosphere through sensory detail (salt, fog, decay, the house's groan)
* Show Elara's grief as different from the others' (she feels a "void in the walls")
* Introduce Victor/Rui as someone who knows more than he should
* Plant the first subliminal tell: he never refers to a specific memory of the house before his "return" — only to the house as it is now
Character beats:
* Elara: Carries herself like a soldier bracing for war; precise, calm speech masking grief
* Victor/Rui: Smooth, polished tone; eyes dart left before a lie; smile never reaches eyes; he is deliberately vague about anything pre-1985
Specific dialogue moments:
* Driver (in Portuguese, subtitled in italics): "This place eats people, senhora."
* Victor: "Amelia said you'd come last. She knew you'd need time to grieve."
* Elara (to Victor, testing): "You must have missed this place, all those years in Brazil."
* Victor (smooth, deflecting): "One misses what one chooses to remember." [His eyes dart left — the tell, planted early]
* Elara (internal): The house isn't empty. It's waiting.
Sensory details to include:
* The smell of salt and rot
* Fog swallowing the sound of waves
* The iron gates screeching open
* The house groaning like a dying beast
Emotional state: Unease settling into the bones; grief as a physical weight
How the scene ends: Elara steps through the threshold. The temperature drops ten degrees. She hears the house exhale.
Transition: Victor leads her inside, his footsteps echoing on marble that once gleamed.

SCENE 2 PROMPT: Grand Foyer
Location: The grand foyer (decaying chandelier, water-stained marble floor, grand staircase) Time of day: Late afternoon, light failing
Who is present: Elara, Victor/Rui, Clara, Ethan, Maria (serving tea), Sophia (seated near a window), James (standing in shadow), Liam (sketching in corner), Dr. Richard (in armchair, clutching his locket), Aunt Inês (observing from staircase)
What happens: The family gathers in the foyer. Clara uses the phrase "step-relation" as a weapon, implying Elara has no blood right to the inheritance. Elara deflects without confirming or denying her connection to Amelia — she doesn't yet know Amelia is her biological aunt. Ethan lingers near the greenhouse doors, watching Elara with an unreadable expression and touching his wrist unconsciously. Maria serves tea (laced with valerian to calm Sophia — Elara does not know this yet). A storm hits; the mansion groans as saltwater floods the east wing. Dr. Richard opens his locket, stares at Sophia's photo, says nothing.
IMPORTANT NOTE FOR THE WRITER: During this foyer scene, establish that James and Inês are the only heirs old enough to have known Victor before his "estrangement." Neither reacts warmly to him — James is stiff and avoids Victor's eyes; Inês is cool and carefully neutral. To a first-time reader this reads as old family tensions. On re-read, it is guilt and knowledge. Do not explain it yet.
What this scene must accomplish:
* Introduce all key family members and their dynamics
* Establish Clara's cruelty and Elara's unresolved question about her own connection to Amelia
* Create the first romantic beat (Elara and Ethan's silent moment)
* Show the mansion as an active, threatening presence
* Plant James's stiffness toward Victor and Inês's careful neutrality
* End with the cliffhanger: the heartbeat in the walls
Character beats:
* Clara: Sharp, theatrical laughter; constantly adjusts her sleeves; her desperation hides beneath bravado
* Ethan: Soft-spoken, watches Elara; touches wrist unconsciously (the tracker)
* Maria: Moves with ritualistic slowness; her envelope is in her apron pocket — she touches it without opening it yet
* James: Stands in shadow, observing everything; his body angles away from Victor
* Inês: On the staircase, watching Victor with an expression that could be contempt or fear
* Dr. Richard: Opens his locket. Closes it. Opens it again. Does not look up.
* Sophia: Ghost-like, speaks in whispers, already fading
Specific dialogue moments:
* Clara: "The step-relation. How quaint that Amelia included you."
* Elara (quiet, not confirming or denying): "Amelia knew who I was to her."
* Ethan (to Elara, very quiet): "You look like you've seen a ghost."
* Elara (to Ethan): "Not yet."
* Maria (to Sophia, in Portuguese, subtitled): "Drink, little one. It will quiet the trembling."
* James (from shadow, to no one in particular): "Thirty years changes a house." [Subtext: It also changes a face. Rui's eyes dart left.]
Sensory details to include:
* The chandelier's crystals clicking against each other (the house is shifting)
* Tea that smells of honey and something deeper (valerian)
* Rain hammering the windows like fists
* Saltwater seeping under the east wing doors
* The house groaning — then a sound beneath it: thump-thump-thump
Emotional state: From curiosity to unease; isolation (Elara is the only one who truly grieves)
How the scene ends: Elara climbs the staircase to her room. The storm intensifies. She hears the heartbeat again.
Transition line: "The mansion swallowed her footsteps as she climbed toward the east wing."

CLIFFHANGER INSTRUCTION for Chapter 1:
End with Elara in her room (decaying bedroom, east wing, wallpaper peeling, single lamp flickering). She hears a rhythmic thump-thump-thump from inside the walls. When she presses her ear to the plaster, it stops. She remembers Amelia's last phone call: "The house is breathing, Elara. Listen for the heartbeat."
The reader should understand: Something is alive inside the mansion's walls. Amelia knew about it. Elara is the only one who can hear it.
The reader should NOT understand: What is causing the sound. Whether it is mechanical, natural, or something else.
Final image: "Elara's ear pressed to cold plaster. Silence. Then — from deep within the wall, so faint she might have imagined it — the house exhaled."

SCENE 1 PROMPT: Dining Room
Location: The formal dining room (long mahogany table, faded tapestries, a chandelier missing three crystals) Time of day: Evening, candles lit (generator is unreliable)
Who is present: Elara, Victor/Rui, Clara, Ethan, Sophia, Liam, Dr. Richard, Inês, James (standing at attention), Maria (serving)
What happens: The first formal dinner. Victor/Rui "accidentally" mentions Clara's embezzlement — $500,000 missing from her modeling agency. Clara slams her fist on the table. Victor smiles. Elara watches the reactions: Liam drops his fork; Sophia's hand trembles; Inês goes very still. Victor has established his power. After dinner, Elara overhears Victor on the phone — she catches only fragments: "Pereira will sign off... the clause requires five... we have time." This is her first hint that the forfeit clause is being actively manipulated.
IMPORTANT: When Victor exits the dining room, Elara glimpses him on his phone. She hears the name "Pereira" and the number "five." She does not yet understand what this means. Plant it clearly enough that readers will connect it later.
What this scene must accomplish:
* Establish Victor's method: public humiliation as control
* Show Clara's desperation
* Create Elara's first moral dilemma: warn Clara or stay silent?
* Plant the forfeit clause and Solicitor Pereira as a named entity
* Show the family's fear
Character beats:
* Victor: Smooth, casual cruelty; enjoying himself
* Clara: Her theatrical mask cracks; she's terrified
* Liam: Drops his fork — his sketchbook is open beside his plate; he quickly closes it (he's been sketching Ethan's wrist)
* Elara (internal): This is how he'll kill them. One secret at a time.
Specific dialogue moments:
* Victor: "Clara, your former accountant called. Something about five hundred thousand? But I'm sure it's a misunderstanding."
* Clara: "You wouldn't dare." (She's already lost)
* Elara (internal): Dare? He's already done it.
* Victor (on phone, Elara overhearing): "...Pereira will sign off. The clause requires five. We have time." [Then, noticing her in the hallway:] "Elara. You've wandered from dinner."
* Victor (smoothly, closing his phone): "Estate business. Dull stuff." [Eyes dart left.]
Sensory details to include:
* The clink of silverware against porcelain (too loud in the silence)
* Clara's fist hitting the table — wine glass tips, red spreads across white linen
* Maria's hands steady as she clears the spill; she touches her apron pocket where the sealed envelope waits
Emotional state: Dread settling in; the sense that no one is safe
How the scene ends: Clara flees the dining room. Victor resumes eating. "She'll calm down." Ethan watches Elara. She looks away first.
Transition: After dinner, Elara follows the greenhouse light.

SCENE 2 PROMPT: Greenhouse
Location: The cliffside greenhouse (bioluminescent medicinal plants, glass ceiling showing storm clouds, a pool of dark water in the center) Time of day: Night, storm still raging
Who is present: Elara, Ethan
What happens: Elara finds Ethan in the greenhouse, examining the bioluminescent plants. She confronts him about his calmness — why isn't he afraid? He brushes her off: "Some of us don't need Amelia's approval to belong here." The words cut. This is the first crack in their bond. As she watches him, she notices he touches his left wrist repeatedly — pressing against it as if checking for a pulse that isn't his own. She notices but cannot name what she is seeing yet.
What this scene must accomplish:
* Create the romantic thread's first wound (Ethan's rejection)
* Show that Ethan has his own agenda
* Establish the greenhouse as an important location
* Plant the wrist detail prominently (tracker foreshadowing)
Character beats:
* Ethan: Soft-spoken, but his words are armor; the wrist-touching is almost involuntary
* Elara: Confused, hurt, but hides it behind precision
Specific dialogue moments:
* Elara: "How are you so calm? People are going to get hurt here."
* Ethan: "People always get hurt. The question is whether you choose it or let it choose you."
* Elara: "That's not an answer."
* Ethan (looking at the bioluminescent pool): "Some of us don't need Amelia's approval to belong here."
* Elara (quiet): "I didn't think you were one of them."
* Ethan (pausing, touching his wrist): "You don't know what I am."
Sensory details to include:
* The bioluminescent plants glowing blue-green in the dark
* Rain hammering the glass ceiling
* The pool's dark water — too still, like it is waiting
* The warmth of the greenhouse against the cold storm outside
Emotional state: Hurt confusion; the beginning of suspicion
How the scene ends: Ethan walks away, leaving Elara alone in the greenhouse. She watches him disappear into the mansion. Then she hears something below her feet — a hollow sound, like the floor is not solid. The tunnels.
Transition: Elara finds a concealed latch behind a trellis of glowing vines.

SCENE 3 PROMPT: The Tunnels
Location: The hidden Prohibition-era tunnels beneath the mansion (flooded in places, saltwater ankle-deep, walls carved with old smugglers' marks and, more recently, with systematic notes in a small, military hand) Time of day: Night, no light except Elara's phone
Who is present: Elara, Liam (clutching his sketchbook)
What happens: Elara follows the sound of movement through the tunnels. She finds Liam, shaking, clutching his sketchbook. He whispers that Victor knows about his addiction and will tell Inês if he doesn't keep reporting. Liam has been sketching who goes where — Victor's eyes in the house. His sketchbook falls into the tunnel water. He dives after it, nearly drowning. Elara pulls him out. In the gloom, she sees Ethan's face watching from the staircase above. He vanishes before she can call out. She also notices the tunnel walls are marked with military shorthand — James has been down here, mapping.
What this scene must accomplish:
* Introduce the tunnels as a key location
* Show that Victor has leverage on Liam
* Establish Liam as Victor's embedded spy (setting up his conscience arc)
* Plant James's tunnel mapping (he's been preparing)
* Create the chapter's cliffhanger (Ethan watching)
Character beats:
* Liam: Nervous, stuttering speech; the sketchbook is his record of Victor's surveillance network — and his conscience
* Elara: Pulled between compassion and suspicion
* Ethan (brief appearance): Silent, watching, then gone
Specific dialogue moments:
* Liam: "Victor knows. About the pills. He said he'd tell Inês. She'd send me away."
* Elara: "What does he want from you?"
* Liam: "To watch. To sketch. Who talks to who. Who goes where. I didn't mean to— " [drops sketchbook]
* Elara (after saving him): "How long have you been doing this?"
* Liam (whispering): "Since we arrived. He had the envelope ready. He knew I'd say yes."
Sensory details to include:
* Cold saltwater soaking through shoes
* The smell of mildew and old stone
