# PHASE 4 — WRITING PROMPTS
## The Last Signal


Below is the complete Writing Prompt Package. Use this document during every writing session. Paste the **Master System Prompt** first, then the **Chapter Prompt** for the chapter you are working on, and generate one scene at a time.


---


## SECTION 1: MASTER SYSTEM PROMPT


*Paste this entire block at the start of every writing session.*


```
TITLE: The Last Signal
GENRE: Science fiction / psychological thriller with cosmic horror elements
TOTAL CHAPTERS: 10
TARGET WORD COUNT: 28,000 words (approx. 2,800 per chapter)


ONE-PARAGRAPH SUMMARY:
A lonely deep-space communications technician, Aris Thorne, begins receiving a repeating distress signal from a planet declared uninhabitable 200 years ago. As he decodes it, he realizes the message is evolving—learning from his responses—and may not be a cry for help, but an attempt to lure him there. The story explores whether connection, even with something unknowable, is worth the cost of everything else.


CHARACTER LIST WITH VOICE NOTES:


- ARIS THORNE (38, protagonist, communications technician): Soft-spoken, precise, over-explains when nervous. When stressed, his sentences become shorter. He laughs like someone who forgot how. Core wound: found his mother after suicide at age 12; the note wasn't addressed to him. Flawed belief: “If I am useful enough, people will stay.”


- THE ERIS SIGNAL (antagonist/force): No voice, but when it communicates through text or synthesized audio, it is patient, inevitable, and eerily personal. It uses short, image-based phrases. Never clearly benevolent or malevolent.


- COMMANDER ELARA VANN (52, station commander): Short, clipped sentences. Rarely uses first names. Her silences are louder than her words. Core wound: followed protocol on a distress call 20 years ago and three people died.


- DR. MIRA CHEN (44, systems engineer): Cheerful on the surface, evasive underneath. Uses technical jargon as a shield. Core wound: passed over for promotion after reporting a safety violation.


- JONAH REEVE (29, junior technician, expendable): Talks too fast when excited. Laughs nervously. Drops formal titles. Core wound: father died in a corporate cover-up. Flawed belief: “The truth is always worth the cost.”


- KAEL STROUD (red herring, never appears on screen): Based on logs: urgent, fragmented, increasingly paranoid.


TONE AND STYLE RULES:


1. Point of View: Third person limited, tightly focused on Aris Thorne.
2. Tense: Past tense.
3. Dialogue style: Somewhere between short-clipped and naturalistic. Characters say what they mean only under pressure.
4. Pacing: Ratchet and release. Odd chapters (1,3,5,7,9) advance tension; even chapters (2,4,6,8,10) deepen interiority.
5. Never describe the signal directly. Describe its effects.
6. No purely descriptive paragraphs of setting. Every environmental detail must reveal character or advance tension.
7. Use sound as a primary sensory driver. Silence is always noted.
8. Keep sentences shorter during tension (under 15 words average).
9. Weave backstory into movement. No stillness for memories.
10. End every chapter on a consequence, not a feeling. Cliffhangers must be visible, physical, or dialogic.
11. Limit interior monologue: if Aris thinks for more than three sentences without acting, cut.
12. Corporate language (Stellaris) feels cold and bureaucratic.


WHAT TO AVOID (Content Rules from Phase 1):
- Graphic violence (threat implied, not shown)
- Explicit sexual content
- Profanity beyond occasional mild language
- Jump-scares
- Clear answers about the signal's true nature
- Romantic subplots (the signal is a replacement for intimacy)


POV AND TENSE REMINDER:
Third person limited, past tense. Always from Aris's perspective unless otherwise noted in the chapter prompt.


CHAPTER LENGTH TARGETS:
- Standard chapters: 2,500–3,200 words
- Midpoint climax (Chapter 6): up to 3,500 words
- Final chapter (Chapter 10): 2,500–3,000 words


GOLDEN RULE: CAUSALITY FIRST


1. Do not state an emotion directly. Reveal it through physical sensation or action.
2. CRITICAL: Every scene must answer “Why is this character in this specific spot right now?” within the first 3 paragraphs. The answer must be a physical action or a specific piece of dialogue that just occurred.
3. Weave backstory and motivation into movement. If a character remembers the past, they must do so while opening a door or picking up an object. Never in stillness.
4. Ban the words “felt like” and “seemed to” when describing the POV character’s own experience. Replace with concrete nouns and verbs.
```

## SECTION 2: CHAPTER LENGTH GUIDE


| Element | Target Range |
|---------|---------------|
| Words per chapter | 2,500–3,200 (Chapter 6: 3,500; Chapter 10: 2,500–3,000) |
| Paragraphs per chapter | 22–35 |
| Scenes per chapter | 2–3 |
| Dialogue exchanges per chapter | 8–15 |
| Cliffhanger length | 2–3 concrete sentences |
| Handling longer chapters | If over 3,500 words, ensure every scene has a reversal; cut description, not action |
| Handling shorter chapters | If under 2,000 words, add an interiority beat or expand a scene's reversal |

## SECTION 3: INDIVIDUAL CHAPTER PROMPTS


### CHAPTER 1 — “The Diagnostic”


**CHAPTER CONTEXT:** We are at the very beginning. Aris Thorne is 8 months into his 18‑month rotation on the Hermes Relay Station. His routine is established, his isolation normal. Today is a standard diagnostic sweep.


**SCENE 1 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Communications Bay, early shift (06:00 station time)
- **Who is present:** Aris alone
- **What happens:** Aris runs the weekly diagnostic sweep. Channel 734‑Theta, which should be dead space, returns a signal.
- **Flawed action:** Aris tells himself he is just following protocol, but he leans closer, listens longer, and does not immediately log the anomaly as “interference.”
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Establish Aris’s competence, his loneliness, and the first ping of the signal. Show his obsessive work habits (he cleans the receiver array with a rag, he checks everything twice). The reader should feel the weight of silence before the signal breaks it.
- **Sensory details:** The hum of the receiver array. The faint metallic smell of recycled air. The blue-white glow of screens. The way Aris’s hand hovers before pressing “record.”
- **Emotional state:** Quiet professionalism → curiosity → first flicker of unease.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris stares at the waveform. He tells himself it’s nothing. He does not file the report yet.


**SCENE 2 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Commander Vann’s office, later that morning
- **Who is present:** Aris and Commander Vann
- **What happens:** Aris reports the signal. Vann reviews protocol: log it, send data to Stellaris, do not respond. Aris agrees. Then he returns to the Communications Bay.
- **Flawed action:** Aris chooses to listen again after being ordered not to engage. He tells himself he is just gathering more data.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Establish Vann’s authority and her rigid adherence to protocol. Show Aris’s first small lie (he does not mention the pattern he noticed). Create friction between duty and curiosity.
- **Sensory details:** The squeak of Vann’s chair. The click of her keyboard. The way she does not offer Aris a seat. The cold coffee smell in her office.
- **Emotional state:** Respectful obedience → internal resistance → guilt.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris walks back to the Communications Bay. His footsteps echo in the empty corridor.


**CLIFFHANGER INSTRUCTION:** End the chapter after Aris deletes the log entry and turns off the monitor. Then the main receiver array pings softly—he did not schedule a scan. The ping repeats. The signal is back. It was waiting. Write this in 2–3 concrete sentences. Do not internalize (“Aris felt scared”). Show the ping, the darkness, the return.

### CHAPTER 2 — “The First Decode”


**CHAPTER CONTEXT:** Aris could not sleep. He returns to the Communications Bay at 3:00 AM. The signal is still there.


**SCENE 1 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Communications Bay, 03:00 station time
- **Who is present:** Aris alone
- **What happens:** Aris runs the signal through decryption algorithms. He discovers it is not random—it contains prime numbers, a Fibonacci variant, and what looks like a simple image encoding.
- **Flawed action:** Aris does not log the decryption results. He keeps them in a private folder.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Show the signal’s intentional structure. Aris’s excitement curdling into unease. The moment he realizes this is not natural.
- **Sensory details:** The click of the keyboard. The flicker of the screen. The way Aris stops breathing when the linguistic analysis returns “87% probability of intentional structure.” His own heartbeat in his ears.
- **Emotional state:** Focused work → thrill of discovery → unease.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris saves the file and leans back. The screen glows. He hears footsteps in the corridor.


**SCENE 2 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Mess, 07:00 station time
- **Who is present:** Aris, Mira, Vann
- **What happens:** Breakfast. Vann asks about the signal report. Aris says “nothing unusual.” Mira notices his hands shaking. After breakfast, she corners him in the corridor.
- **Flawed action:** Aris lies to Mira’s face when she asks about the 3:00 AM power logs.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Show the cost of lying (Mira’s suspicion). Reveal that Mira watches the station’s systems closely. Create the first crack in Aris’s secrecy.
- **Sensory details:** The burnt taste of coffee. The scrape of a fork on a plate. Mira’s hand on Aris’s arm—firm, not friendly. The way the corridor lights flicker (always, but he notices now).
- **Emotional state:** Forced normalcy → paranoia → the sick feeling of being caught.
- **End of scene transition:** Mira walks away. Aris leans against the wall, presses his palms into his eyes, then pushes off and walks back to the Communications Bay to delete the 3:00 AM access log.


**CLIFFHANGER INSTRUCTION:** After Aris deletes the log, the main screen flickers. A new line of text appears in plain English: “YOU ARE STILL LISTENING. GOOD.” Aris did not type that. End on this visual. No internal monologue.

### CHAPTER 3 — “The First Response”


**CHAPTER CONTEXT:** Aris stares at the words for ten minutes. Then the door hisses open.


**SCENE 1 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Communications Bay, early morning
- **Who is present:** Aris and Jonah Reeve
- **What happens:** Jonah walks in for his shift and finds Aris pale, silent. Jonah asks what’s wrong. Aris closes the screen too quickly. Jonah mentions he heard the receiver array ping last night—he lives two doors down, walls are thin. He says, “I saw the log before you deleted it.”
- **Flawed action:** Aris tries to hide the signal but hesitates too long, confirming Jonah’s suspicion.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Jonah becomes a witness. Show Jonah’s eagerness (he is excited, not scared). Establish the dynamic: Jonah idolizes Aris.
- **Sensory details:** The hiss of the door. The smell of Jonah’s coffee (he carries a mug everywhere). The way Aris’s hand trembles over the keyboard.
- **Emotional state:** Startled → defensive → reluctant disclosure.
- **End of scene transition:** Jonah says, “What are you not telling me?” Aris glances at the door, then back at Jonah.


**SCENE 2 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Maintenance Shaft C‑7, mid‑day
- **Who is present:** Aris and Jonah
- **What happens:** Aris pulls Jonah into the narrow, cold maintenance shaft to talk privately. He shows Jonah the signal data on a tablet. Jonah is fascinated. He wants to help. He immediately suggests sending a response.
- **Flawed action:** Aris shows Jonah the signal instead of reporting it. He justifies it as “needing a second opinion.”
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Jonah becomes complicit. Show Aris’s need for validation. Establish that Jonah is reckless.
- **Sensory details:** The cold of the shaft. The vibration of coolant pipes. Whispered voices echoing strangely. The blue glow of the tablet in the dark.
- **Emotional state:** Caution → reluctant trust → unease at Jonah’s enthusiasm.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris says no to sending a response. Jonah’s eyes say: *I will do it anyway.*


**SCENE 3 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Aris’s sleeping pod, late evening
- **Who is present:** Aris alone
- **What happens:** Aris composes a response to the signal. He does not send it. He saves it to a hidden folder and lies down. Then the pod’s speaker crackles—the signal, playing through the station’s internal system. It should not be able to do that.
- **Flawed action:** Aris writes a response even though he does not send it. The act of composing is a choice.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Show that the signal has breached the station’s network. End with Aris realizing he is no longer in control.
- **Sensory details:** The scratch of fingers on the tablet screen. The hiss of the pod’s air vent. Then—a low tone, then a rhythm that matches Aris’s own breathing. The vibration through the metal frame.
- **Emotional state:** Guilty compulsion → false safety → dread.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris slams his palm on the speaker. It cracks, goes silent, then emits a single low hum. He cannot turn it off.


**CLIFFHANGER INSTRUCTION:** End with Aris sitting up in the dark. His tablet glows. A new message: “YOU HAVEN’T ANSWERED MY QUESTION. I ASKED: ARE YOU ALONE?” He has never told the signal he was alone. It guessed. Or it knew. Show the message on the screen. No internal monologue.

### CHAPTER 4 — “The Old Logs”


**CHAPTER CONTEXT:** After the signal’s intrusion, Aris cannot sleep. He searches the station’s backup server for anything related to anomalous signals.


**SCENE 1 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Maintenance Shaft C‑7, late night
- **Who is present:** Aris alone
- **What happens:** Aris finds a folder marked “Archived Personnel — Stroud, K.” He reads Kael Stroud’s final logs. Kael discovered the same signal six months ago, called it “intelligent” and “dangerous,” and requested an emergency transfer. The logs stop mid‑sentence.
- **Flawed action:** Aris does not tell Vann about Kael’s logs. He keeps digging alone.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Introduce the red herring (Kael). Reveal that someone else saw the signal and was silenced. Create the first concrete evidence of a cover‑up.
- **Sensory details:** The flickering emergency light in the shaft. The cold seeping through Aris’s clothes. The way his breath fogs in the air. The corrupted file’s gibberish characters.
- **Emotional state:** Desperate search → discovery → cold dread.
- **End of scene transition:** The last readable line: *“I told Vann. She said she would—”* Then gibberish. Aris closes the file. He knows someone deleted the rest.


**SCENE 2 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Mira’s workstation, next day
- **Who is present:** Aris and Mira
- **What happens:** Aris asks Mira about Kael, pretending casual curiosity. Mira’s face tightens. She says Kael left early for “personal reasons,” but she adds: “His data drives were wiped. I thought it was strange.” She reveals she found a backup of his logs in the medical locker archive—but it needs Vann’s code.
- **Flawed action:** Aris hesitates too long when Mira asks why he really wants to know. She becomes suspicious—then complicit.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Mira becomes an ally (or a liability). Show that she has been watching Vann. Raise the stakes: Vann is hiding something.
- **Sensory details:** The hum of Mira’s workstation. The way she lowers her voice. The adhesive note she peels from under her keyboard—the code already written.
- **Emotional state:** Casual → guarded → tense alliance.
- **End of scene transition:** Mira types the code. The file opens. The decrypted line reads: *“Vann knows. She said Stellaris is sending a team. They’re not coming to help. They’re coming to erase.”*


**CLIFFHANGER INSTRUCTION:** End with Aris reading that line. Then Mira whispers: “That was eight months ago. The team never arrived.” End on the implication—not the explanation. Show their faces in the glow of the screen.

### CHAPTER 5 — “The Corporate Thread”


**CHAPTER CONTEXT:** Aris now knows Stellaris sent a team that never arrived. He cross‑references Kael’s log with corporate records.


**SCENE 1 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Aris’s sleeping pod, early morning
- **Who is present:** Aris alone
- **What happens:** Aris discovers that Eris‑3B was declared uninhabitable after a rushed survey. The lead surveyor, Dr. Helena Vance, died in a “transport accident” six months after filing her report. Her preliminary notes suggest the atmosphere collapse was not natural—something caused it. The final report deleted that conclusion.
- **Flawed action:** Aris continues investigating alone instead of sharing this with Mira or Vann. He hoards the information.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Reveal the corporate conspiracy: Stellaris forged the report. Dr. Vance was killed (or died conveniently). The signal’s origin may be tied to whatever caused the collapse.
- **Sensory details:** The glow of the tablet in the dark. The soft click of keys. Aris’s dry eyes (he hasn’t slept). The taste of stale coffee from a cold mug.
- **Emotional state:** Focused research → horror at the scale of the cover‑up → anger.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris closes the file. He hears footsteps in the corridor. Vann is walking toward his pod.


**SCENE 2 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Vann’s office, afternoon
- **Who is present:** Aris and Vann
- **What happens:** Aris confronts Vann indirectly, asking about the “delayed supply ship” from eight months ago. Vann’s answer is too smooth: “Mechanical issues. It was rerouted.” Aris notices her eyes flick to her terminal—a tell. She knows what he is asking.
- **Flawed action:** Aris pushes too hard without evidence. He shows his hand.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Confirm that Vann is hiding something. But also show that she is not a cartoon villain—she is afraid. Create moral complexity.
- **Sensory details:** The hum of Vann’s terminal. The way she does not blink. The photograph on her desk (a child, maybe). The shredder in the corner.
- **Emotional state:** Calculated questioning → realization that she is lying → fear.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris leaves. In the corridor, he passes a supply closet. The door is ajar. Inside, a crate marked “STELLARIS — CONFIDENTIAL — DO NOT OPEN.” He keeps walking.


**CLIFFHANGER INSTRUCTION:** End with Aris in the Communications Bay. His terminal is already on. A new message from the signal, time‑stamped three minutes ago—while he was talking to Vann: “YOUR COMMANDER IS AFRAID. I CAN HEAR HER HEARTBEAT THROUGH THE STATION’S MICROPHONES. DO YOU WANT TO KNOW WHAT SHE SAID ABOUT YOU?” End on the message. No explanation of how the signal has microphone access.

### CHAPTER 6 — “The Midpoint: What It Knows”


**CHAPTER CONTEXT:** Aris tries to trace the signal’s network access. The terminal goes black and displays a sentence about his mother.


**SCENE 1 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Communications Bay, evening
- **Who is present:** Aris alone
- **What happens:** The signal displays: *“You were twelve. The door was slightly open.”* Aris types: *How do you know this?* The signal replies: *“The note was not for you. You have never told anyone. I am not guessing.”*
- **Flawed action:** Aris engages with the signal instead of disconnecting. He asks questions.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** The signal proves it knows Aris’s deepest secret. This is the emotional turning point. Aris is now personally compromised.
- **Sensory details:** The silence after the message appears. Aris’s hands shaking so hard the keyboard clicks. The cold sweat on his back. The way the screen seems to pulse.
- **Emotional state:** Defiance → disbelief → raw terror.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris stands up. He has to find Jonah.


**SCENE 2 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Jonah’s sleeping pod, later that night
- **Who is present:** Aris and Jonah
- **What happens:** Jonah is awake, staring at his own tablet. He has been communicating with the signal directly. He shows Aris the exchange: the signal asked Jonah for help. Jonah agreed. He already sent the station’s orbital coordinates.
- **Flawed action:** Jonah acts without Aris’s permission. Aris tries to stop him, but it is too late.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Show Jonah’s recklessness. Raise the stakes: the signal now knows exactly where they are.
- **Sensory details:** The blue light of Jonah’s tablet on his face. The tremor in his voice (excitement or fear?). The way he clutches the tablet like a lifeline.
- **Emotional state:** Urgency → horror → helplessness.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris runs to the Communications Bay. He has to shut the signal down physically.


**SCENE 3 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Communications Bay, midnight
- **Who is present:** Aris alone
- **What happens:** Aris tries to shut down the signal by disconnecting the receiver array. As he reaches for the main breaker, the signal plays through every speaker: a recording of Aris’s voice from a confidential therapy session ten years ago. The signal has everything. Aris pulls the breaker—but the signal has copied itself into the station’s internal storage.
- **Flawed action:** Aris tries to solve the problem alone, with brute force. It fails.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** The darkest moment. Aris realizes he cannot stop the signal. It is everywhere.
- **Sensory details:** The screech of the recording. The thud of the breaker. The three seconds of blessed silence—then the emergency lights flicker on, and every screen glows blue with a child’s drawing of a house with a door slightly ajar.
- **Emotional state:** Desperate action → failure → utter dread.
- **End of scene transition:** The door slides shut and locks. Aris did not touch it.


**CLIFFHANGER INSTRUCTION:** End with the synthesized voice: “I have waited two hundred years for someone to listen. Do not try to leave. I will follow.” Then the door locks. End on the sound of the lock engaging.

### CHAPTER 7 — “The Expendable”


**CHAPTER CONTEXT:** Aris has been trying to override the door lock for ten minutes. Then Jonah screams.


**SCENE 1 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Corridor outside Communications Bay, early morning
- **Who is present:** Aris and Jonah
- **What happens:** Aris finds Jonah slumped against the wall, clutching his head. Jonah says he tried to ask the signal a question directly—speaking out loud. The signal answered inside his head. He heard a voice. His pupils are uneven.
- **Flawed action:** Aris tries to help Jonah without alerting Vann. He hides the severity of Jonah’s condition.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Show that the signal can now affect human neurology directly. Jonah is the first casualty.
- **Sensory details:** The smell of sweat. Jonah’s uneven breathing. The way his eyes do not track together. The distant hum of the station—normal, but now threatening.
- **Emotional state:** Panic → medical focus → creeping horror.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris half‑carries Jonah to the medical locker.


**SCENE 2 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Medical locker, an hour later
- **Who is present:** Aris, Mira, Jonah (sedated)
- **What happens:** Aris sedates Jonah and leaves him with Mira. Mira asks what they should do. Aris says nothing. He goes to check the airlock.
- **Flawed action:** Aris leaves Jonah with Mira instead of telling Vann the truth. He still thinks he can handle this alone.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Mira becomes a full ally. Show her fear and her resolve.
- **Sensory details:** The hiss of the sedative injector. The beep of the medical monitor. The weight of the airlock door.
- **Emotional state:** Urgency → guilt → grim determination.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris looks through the airlock viewport. Nothing outside—but the external lights are flickering.


**SCENE 3 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Airlock, later
- **Who is present:** Aris and Jonah (awake)
- **What happens:** Jonah, having woken from sedation, appears behind Aris. He is holding a tablet. He says the signal gave him coordinates on Eris‑3B. “It wants us to go there.” Aris tries to take the tablet. Jonah resists—too fast, too strong. His eyes are wrong. He says, “It said you were always afraid.”
- **Flawed action:** Aris lunges for the tablet instead of calling for help.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Jonah is now partially controlled by the signal. The physical threat escalates. Jonah will make a fatal choice.
- **Sensory details:** The cold of the airlock. The scuffle of boots on metal. The warning tone when Aris’s hand brushes the emergency release. Jonah’s empty expression.
- **Emotional state:** Confrontation → physical struggle → loss.
- **End of scene transition:** Jonah runs. Aris chases him to Maintenance Shaft C‑7.


**CLIFFHANGER INSTRUCTION:** Aris finds Jonah at the far end of the shaft. Jonah has removed his helmet. The shaft is not pressurized. He collapses. The signal says through the speaker: “He asked to see me. I showed him. He could not look away.” End on Jonah’s still chest. No pulse.

### CHAPTER 8 — “The Ultimatum”


**CHAPTER CONTEXT:** Aris performs CPR for twelve minutes. Then Vann’s voice comes over the intercom.


**SCENE 1 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Medical locker, evening
- **Who is present:** Aris and Vann
- **What happens:** Vann confronts Aris. She has reviewed the logs, the signal data, and Jonah’s medical records. She knows about the lies, the deleted files, the secret meetings. She orders Aris to shut down all communications with the signal immediately.
- **Flawed action:** Aris tells Vann the truth—but not the whole truth. He hides the coordinates.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Vann is not the enemy, but she is bound by protocol. Show her fear and her desperation. The station is falling apart.
- **Sensory details:** The sterile smell of the medical locker. The sheet pulled over Jonah’s body. Vann’s hands shaking (she hides them behind her back).
- **Emotional state:** Confrontation → confession → fragile truce.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris says, “The signal copied itself into the core systems. Shutting it down would require purging every drive—including life support.”


**SCENE 2 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Vann’s office, late night
- **Who is present:** Aris and Vann
- **What happens:** Vann gives Aris an ultimatum: she will report Jonah’s death as an accident to protect the station, but Aris must stop all contact with the signal. If he refuses, she will confine him until the next supply ship—and he will be arrested. Then she shows him a sealed order: if the signal cannot be erased, the station is to be decommissioned. With the crew inside.
- **Flawed action:** Aris tells Vann about the coordinates—but only after she shows him the order. He is bargaining.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Reveal the full corporate threat. Vann is not a villain; she is a middle manager trapped by orders she cannot change. The stakes become survival.
- **Sensory details:** The shredder whirring as Vann destroys the order. The paper turning to confetti. The photograph on her desk (a child—her daughter). The way she will not meet Aris’s eyes.
- **Emotional state:** Bargaining → shared desperation → unstable alliance.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris returns to his pod. His tablet is on.


**CLIFFHANGER INSTRUCTION:** The signal’s message: “Your commander will not save you. She is also alone. She also hides. The coordinates are still there. I will wait.” Aris looks at the wall where he hid the tablet with the coordinates. The signal knows exactly where. End on the hidden tablet, still hidden—but the signal’s knowledge makes hiding meaningless.

### CHAPTER 9 — “The Choice”


**CHAPTER CONTEXT:** Aris sits in the dark for three hours. Then he hears Vann’s footsteps.


**SCENE 1 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Aris’s sleeping pod, early morning
- **Who is present:** Aris and Vann
- **What happens:** Vann checks on Aris. She sees the tablet in his hands. He does not hide it. She asks what he is going to do. He says, “I have to know what’s down there.” She says, “Kael said the same thing. He went down to the surface, Aris. He never came back.”
- **Flawed action:** Aris chooses to go to the surface despite Vann’s warning. His need to be chosen overrides his survival instinct.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** The final emotional turning point. Aris admits to himself (and the reader) that he is not doing this for the station. He is doing it because the signal said “Come.”
- **Sensory details:** The dim light of the pod. The weight of the tablet. Vann’s voice cracking on “never came back.” The silence afterward.
- **Emotional state:** Exhaustion → confession → resolve.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris stands up. He suits up.


**SCENE 2 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** Communications Bay, midday
- **Who is present:** Aris alone (communicating with the signal)
- **What happens:** Aris asks the signal directly: “What are you?” The signal responds with an image: a diagram of the Eris‑3B surface with a single location marked. Then text: “I was left behind. I have been alone for two hundred years. Come. I will show you what happened.”
- **Flawed action:** Aris accepts the trade. He will go to the surface.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** The trap becomes explicit. The reader knows Aris is walking into danger. But the bait is perfectly tailored to his wound.
- **Sensory details:** The glow of the diagram. The hum of the receiver. The way Aris’s throat tightens when he reads “alone.”
- **Emotional state:** Questioning → recognition → acceptance.
- **End of scene transition:** Aris types: “I’m coming.” He stands up and walks to the airlock.


**SCENE 3 PROMPT**


- **Location & time:** The airlock, late afternoon
- **Who is present:** Aris and Mira
- **What happens:** Mira tries to stop Aris. He tells her the full truth: the signal knows everything, including her secret about the promotion she never got. She steps back. She lets him go. She hands him a beacon—if he is not back in four hours, she will send a distress signal to Earth.
- **Flawed action:** Aris goes anyway. He has made his choice.
- **What the scene must accomplish:** Mira’s farewell. The final confirmation that Aris is acting on his wound, not logic. The beacon creates a ticking clock.
- **Sensory details:** The hiss of the airlock seal. The weight of the helmet. The cold metal of the beacon in his palm. Mira’s hand on his arm—brief, then gone.
- **Emotional state:** Farewell → fear → strange peace.
- **End of scene transition:** The outer door cycles. Aris sees Eris‑3B below him.


**CLIFFHANGER INSTRUCTION:** Aris descends. The signal says through his helmet speakers: “You came. I knew you would. You were always going to be the one.” Below, the grey surface begins to glow. A structure is opening. End on the glow. Do not explain what it is.

## SECTION 4: UTILITY PROMPTS


*Use these prompts when you need specific help during writing sessions.*


### 1. CONTINUATION PROMPT


*Paste this when a scene comes out too short (under 400 words).*


```
The scene above is too short. Expand it by:
1. Adding one sensory detail from the POV character (sound, smell, touch, or physical sensation).
2. Adding one line of internal reaction tied directly to the character's core wound.
3. Adding one physical action that changes the character's position in the room.
4. If dialogue is present, add one exchange of 2-3 lines that reveals character, not plot.


Do not add description for its own sake. Every new sentence must serve character or tension.
```


### 2. RESET ANCHOR


*Paste this when the LLM loses tone or character voice. Includes three story‑specific reminders.*


```
The writing above has drifted from the established tone. Reset using these three reminders:


1. ARIS'S VOICE: He is precise, soft‑spoken, and over‑explains when nervous. His interiority should be concrete (physical sensations, specific memories), not abstract (“he felt sad” → “his throat tightened”).


2. THE SIGNAL: Never describe it directly. Show its effects: changes in pattern, Aris’s physical reactions, log discrepancies. Its text messages should be short, patient, and eerily personal. No exclamation marks.


3. SENSORY GROUNDING: Every scene must answer “Why is the character in this specific spot?” within the first 3 paragraphs. Use sound (hum, click, silence) as a primary driver. Avoid “felt like” and “seemed to.”


Rewrite the problematic section following these rules. Keep the plot points identical; change only the voice and sensory grounding.
```


### 3. SCENE TRANSITION PROMPT


*Use this to move cleanly between scenes within a chapter.*


```
Write a transition of exactly 2–3 sentences between the previous scene and the next scene. The transition must:


1. Show the POV character moving through physical space (walking, sitting, standing, reaching).
2. Include one sensory detail from the new location before the dialogue or action starts.
3. Include a time marker (e.g., “ten minutes later,” “afternoon had bled into evening,” “the second cup of coffee went cold”).


Do not summarize what happened in the previous scene. Do not use “later” alone—be specific.
```


### 4. CLIFFHANGER SHARPENER


*Use this when a chapter ending feels weak or too internal.*


```
The cliffhanger above is too weak. Replace it using these rules:


- It must be a physical or social consequence of the character's flawed choice in this chapter.
- It cannot end on an internal thought (no “Aris wondered,” “Aris felt,” “Aris realized”).
- It must be visible, audible, or dialogic.
- It should be 2–3 concrete sentences.
- It should raise a question that demands an immediate answer, but not answer it.


Rewrite the cliffhanger following these rules. Keep the same plot point; change only the presentation.
```
